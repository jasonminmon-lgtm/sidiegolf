📖 How to Use SidieGolf
🚀 Starting a Round ▼
  • Manager opens the app and taps Start a Round.
  • Choose a round type: Stroke Play, Match Play, or Golf Event (multi-day).
  • Select a scoring method (e.g. Net Stroke Play). For Golf Events, choose Ryder Cup for team match play.
  • Pick a course and date. Then add players — each player gets their own tee box assignment (useful when different players play different tees).
  • Enter each player's name and Handicap Index (HI). Tap 🔍 to look up via GHIN, or use 👥 From Buddies to quickly add saved players.
  • Add side games with stakes (Nassau, Wolf, Skins, etc.).
  • Share the join code so playing partners can join. Lock the round when everyone is in.
💡 A 6-digit round code is generated at setup. The same code works for all days of a multi-day event.
📱 Joining a Round ▼
Two ways to join:
  • Watch Live (anonymous): enter the code in the Watch Live box — no sign-in needed. View-only access to the leaderboard and ledger.
  • Sign in + Join: sign in with Google, then enter the code. You'll see the full leaderboard and can claim the scorekeeper role for your group.
💡 For multi-day events the same join code works every day — rejoin with the same code on Day 2 and Day 3.
🏆 Golf Events (Ryder Cup) ▼
Golf Events are multi-day, multi-group tournaments — perfect for golf trips.
  • Choose Ryder Cup as the scoring method for team match play across multiple days.
  • Assign players to Team A (e.g. USA) and Team B (e.g. Europe) during setup.
  • Add the number of days — course, groups, and match format are set each morning from the Event Hub, not upfront. Things can change daily.
  • After locking the round, the Event Hub shows each day as a card. Tap Set Up → on the day of play to pick the course, assign players to tee times, and choose match format (Foursomes / Four-Ball / Singles).
  • Each group gets a live match banner on the scorecard showing the current hole-by-hole standing.
  • The leaderboard shows running Ryder Cup points for both teams across all days.
  • When a day is done, tap ✓ Mark Day Complete — a shareable results summary is generated automatically.
  • After all days, tap Event Results to share the final standings with the whole group.
💡 Each day's format can be completely different — Foursomes on Day 1, Four-Ball on Day 2, Singles on Day 3.
📋 Scorekeepers ▼
For networked rounds and events, each group needs a designated scorekeeper — the manager doesn't have to score every group.
  • Before the round: the manager can self-assign from the Lock & Start lobby. Each group shows an I'll Score button.
  • After joining: signed-in players see a 📋 Scorekeeper panel on the leaderboard. Tap I'll Score to claim the scorekeeper role for your group.
  • Only the claimed scorekeeper (and the manager) can enter scores for that group. Everyone else is view-only.
  • Tap Release to unclaim if someone else should take over.
  • For multi-day events, scorekeeper assignments reset each day — each group re-claims their role fresh each morning.
💡 Watch Live (anonymous) viewers never see the scorekeeper panel — they have read-only access only.
📁 My Courses ▼
Save frequently played courses so you don't have to search for them every round.
  • Tap 📁 My Courses on the home screen to manage your saved courses.
  • Search for any course and tap Save to My Courses — it stores the full tee data (slope, rating, stroke index).
  • On the course selection step, your saved courses appear at the top for one-tap selection.
  • Saved courses are tied to your account and available across devices.
💡 Private or custom courses can be entered manually and saved for future rounds.
⛳ Entering Scores ▼
  • Tap any score cell on the scorecard to enter a score.
  • Quick buttons appear for Eagle, Birdie, Par, Bogey, Double, Triple.
  • Use the +/− stepper at the bottom to enter any other score.
  • The leaderboard and money ledger update in real time across all connected devices.
  • When done, tap Finish & Save Round to record it in history.
🤝 Nassau ▼
Nassau is three bets in one: Front 9, Back 9, and Total 18. Each bet is settled separately.
  • Played net (after handicap strokes) by default.
  • In a 1v1 Nassau, strokes are based on just those two players — not the full group.
  • Auto-press: a new side bet automatically starts when you're down by 1 or 2 holes.
  • Carry ties: if the front 9 ends in a tie, the front 9 bet carries over to the back 9.
💡 You can have multiple Nassau matches in one round — e.g. Jason vs Tim and Jason vs Jay are separate games.
🐺 Wolf ▼
The Wolf rotates each hole based on the tee order.
  • After watching tee shots, Wolf picks a partner — or goes Lone Wolf (2×) for double stakes.
  • Blind Wolf: Wolf calls it before any tee shots are hit — pays out at 3× if they win.
  • Winners collect $ Per Man from every loser individually.
  • Set tee order in the Wolf game card — it determines who is Wolf on each hole.
💡 Recommend using 80% handicap allowance for Wolf since it's a team game.
🔨 Hammer Wolf ▼
Same as Wolf with one addition: anyone can throw a hammer at any point during a hole to double the current bet.
  • Hammers stack — a hole could be worth many times the starting bet.
  • The hammered side can decline instead of playing out the hole — they forfeit at the pre-hammer rate.
🪙 Coin Flip Wolf ▼
A Wolf variation for 4 or 5 players — no rotating wolf, no tee order. Every player flips Heads or Tails on every hole. Same flip = partners.
  • Teams: 2v2 or 1v3 (4 players) · 2v3 or 1v4 (5 players).
  • Flat $ per man — no solo double. A lone 1v3 player who wins collects $X from each of the 3 opponents.
  • Solo holds the hammer: the lone player's side must throw first. Hammers stack; declining forfeits at the current rate.
  • Ghost Partner (optional): a solo player gets a phantom partner who scores net par. The solo uses the better of their own net vs par. Throwing a hammer forfeits the ghost that hole.
  • Everyone for Themselves: if all players flip the same, bet doubles, hammer is fully open (any player), and lowest net score wins. Any tie = push/carry.
💡 In the per-hole table: "Wolf side" = H-team (or solo team), "Opp side" = T-team. Scorecard cells are shaded blue (H) or orange (T).
💰 Skins ▼
Win a hole outright (lowest score in the group) to claim a skin worth $ Per Skin.
  • Score Basis: choose Gross to play off raw scores, or Net to play off handicap-adjusted scores. When Gross is selected the handicap allowance slider is hidden since it has no effect.
  • Ties carry the pot forward to the next hole (if carryover is on).
  • A carried skin can grow large if several holes tie in a row.
  • If the final hole ties, the pot is split or voided depending on your house rules.
💡 For a group-level skins game, use Skins. For a tournament-wide pot across all tee times, use Skins (Event-Wide).
🏅 Skins (Event-Wide) ▼
A tournament-wide skins pot for large groups spanning multiple tee times. Every player buys in — the total pot is split equally among all skins won across the full field.
  • Buy-In Per Player ($): everyone contributes this amount. Total pot = buy-in × number of players.
  • Score Basis: Gross (raw scores) or Net (handicap-adjusted). Gross is most common for tournament skins.
  • Carryover: ties can carry the skin to the next hole, building bigger prizes.
  • Payouts are proportional — win more skins, collect a larger share of the pot minus your buy-in.
💡 Add Skins (Event-Wide) from the Event-Wide Games section on the Side Games step — it appears there because it spans all tee times, not just one group.
🎯 Quota ▼
Each player earns Stableford points per hole relative to par.
  • Standard: Eagle=4, Birdie=3, Par=2, Bogey=1, Double+=0.
  • Each player has a quota target based on their handicap (36 minus course handicap).
  • Settle up: pay/collect $ Per Point based on how far above/below quota you finish.
  • Buy-in pot option: pool a set amount and pay out to top finishers.
🐍 Snake ▼
Nobody wants to hold the snake at the end of the round.
  • A player "takes the snake" for an infraction (typically a 3-putt).
  • The snake transfers each time someone else commits the same infraction.
  • Whoever holds the snake at the end of 18 pays the group $ Per Snake.
  • Mark who has the snake each hole directly on the scorecard.
🎲 Other Games ▼
Bingo Bango Bongo — Three points per hole:
  • Bingo: first ball on the green.
  • Bango: closest to the pin once all balls are on the green.
  • Bongo: first ball in the hole.
9 Point (Baseball / 5-3-1) — A threesome game. Each hole's 9 points split by net score:
  • Low score = 5, middle = 3, high = 1.
  • Ties share the combined points of their spots: two tie low → 4/4/1, two tie high → 5/2/2, three-way tie → 3/3/3.
  • 2-stroke sweep: beat both opponents by 2+ net strokes to take all 9.
  • Birdies Double (option): a gross birdie doubles every point on the hole; a gross eagle triples it (stacks with the sweep — a gross birdie vs two bogeys = 18).
  • Cry Baby (option): on the closing holes (16-18 or 17-18) the trailing player sets a higher $/point as a comeback lever.
Vegas — Team scores are combined into a 2-digit number (lower score first). The difference in those numbers is the point swing. High swings happen when one team has a big mismatch (e.g. birdie + double).

Round Robin (2-man teams) — Partners rotate every 6 holes. Settle up per 6-hole segment based on best-ball score.
♟️ Handicaps & Allowances ▼
  • Enter each player's Handicap Index (HI) — this is your official GHIN number.
  • The app converts HI to a Course Handicap using the course's slope and rating.
  • Strokes are distributed across holes by stroke index (SI 1 = hardest hole).
  • For individual matches (Nassau), strokes are anchored to the lowest HI in that match only.
  • For group games (Wolf, Skins), strokes are anchored to the lowest HI in the full group.
Handicap % Allowance slider:
  • 100% = full handicap (default for Nassau and individual games).
  • 80% = recommended for Wolf and team games.
  • 0% = scratch play, no strokes given.
👥 My Buddies ▼
Save your regular playing partners so you can add them to a round in one tap.
  • Tap 👥 My Buddies on the home screen to manage your list — add, view, or delete buddies.
  • Each buddy stores their name, GHIN number, and Handicap Index.
  • On the player entry step, tap 👥 From Buddies to pick a saved player and auto-fill their slot.
  • When you manually enter a player with a GHIN, the app will prompt you to save them as a buddy for next time.
💡 Your buddy list is tied to your account and available every round — no re-entering names and handicaps.
📍 Course Search & GPS ▼
  • Tap 📍 Near Me on the course step to automatically search for courses at your current location.
  • Your browser will ask for location permission — tap Allow for GPS to work.
  • The app detects your city and searches GolfCourseAPI for nearby courses with real tee data (rating, slope, par, stroke index).
  • You can also search by name (e.g. "Barton Creek") if you prefer.
  • Selecting a course from search loads real tee data so handicap strokes are calculated correctly.
💡 GPS works best when you're at or near the course. If Near Me returns too many results, search by the course name directly.
💵 Settle Up ▼
After the round, tap the Settle Up button on the leaderboard.
  • The app calculates the minimum number of transactions to settle all debts.
  • Each player can enter their Venmo handle (e.g. @jason-inmon).
  • Tap Pay on Venmo to open Venmo with the amount and recipient pre-filled.
  • Tap Share Ledger to copy or share the full settlement summary.
💡 Venmo handles are saved so you don't have to re-enter them next round.
